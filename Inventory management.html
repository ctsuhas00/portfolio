Featured Project

Smart Warehouse Management System

An RFID and ESP32 based system that automates product identification, verification, and inventory tracking across a warehouse floor.

Completed Duration: 3 weeks
ESP32 RFID (MFRC522) IR Sensors Servo Motors LCD Display Wi-Fi

Project Overview

A warehouse automation project that combines RFID identification with ESP32-based control to track products as they move through a facility, reducing manual counting and verification work.

Problem Statement

Warehouses often face challenges in manually tracking inventory and verifying products, leading to errors, delays, and inefficient management.

Objectives

  • Automate product identification using RFID tags
  • Track inventory movement in real time
  • Reduce manual verification effort and human error
  • Sort verified vs unverified products automatically

Working Principle

Each product is tagged with an RFID chip. As it passes the MFRC522 reader, the ESP32 verifies the tag against a known inventory list, updates the count, and signals a servo-based sorting mechanism to route the product accordingly, while IR sensors confirm physical movement along the line.

System Architecture

The ESP32 acts as the central controller, reading RFID tag data over SPI, processing verification logic, driving a servo motor for sorting, and displaying live status on an LCD, with Wi-Fi connectivity reserved for future remote monitoring.
