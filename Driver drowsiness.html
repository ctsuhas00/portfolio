Featured Project

Driver Drowsiness Detection System

An ESP32-based safety system that monitors driver alertness in real time and intervenes automatically if drowsiness is detected.

Completed Duration: 2 weeks
ESP32 IR Sensors Buzzer L298N Motor Driver Wi-Fi

Project Overview

A road-safety focused embedded system designed to catch early signs of driver fatigue and respond before a lapse in attention becomes dangerous.

Problem Statement

Driver fatigue is a major cause of road accidents; drivers often fail to recognize their own drowsiness in time to react safely.

Objectives

  • Continuously monitor driver eye status
  • Trigger immediate audible and visual alerts on drowsiness
  • Automatically reduce vehicle speed if the driver doesn't respond
  • Activate emergency indicators as a last-resort safety measure

Working Principle

IR sensors continuously track eye-blink patterns. When prolonged eye closure is detected, the ESP32 immediately triggers a buzzer and LED alert. If the driver fails to respond within a set window, the system engages the L298N motor driver to gradually reduce speed and activates emergency indicators.

System Architecture

The ESP32 polls the IR sensor array on a tight loop, runs a simple thresholding algorithm to flag drowsiness, and branches into either an alert routine or an escalated safety routine that talks to the motor driver and indicator circuit.
